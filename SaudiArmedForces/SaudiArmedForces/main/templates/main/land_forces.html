Overview

Royal Saudi Land Forces

The Royal Saudi Land Forces are one of the main branches of the Saudi Armed Forces and represent the first organized military force in the Kingdom. They are responsible for defending the nation’s territory, protecting its interests from external threats, and carrying out ground military operations with strength, discipline, and coordination.

Over the years, the Land Forces have evolved into a modern and advanced military branch, equipped with sophisticated armored vehicles, artillery systems, tactical weapons, and specialized support units that ensure operational readiness across various environments. They are strategically distributed across eight military regions covering the entire geographical scope of Saudi Arabia, and consist of regional commands, independent branches, colleges, institutes, advanced training schools, formations, and administrative bodies.

In addition to border defense, the Land Forces play a vital role in strategic planning, rapid deployment, and joint operations with other branches of the armed forces. They also work in close coordination with national entities to enhance overall security, while optimizing the use of resources through a merit-based operational environment. This integration contributes significantly to maintaining national stability and strengthening regional security.

Military History

History of the Royal Saudi Land Forces

18th Century

Foundations

The roots of the Royal Saudi Land Forces go back to the mid-18th century during the establishment of the First Saudi State. At that time, there was no regular standing army, but a force known as the “Jihad Army” was called upon whenever needed to defend the state and maintain stability.

1902

The Beginning of a Modern Force

With the recapture of Riyadh in 1902, the foundations of an organized military force began to take shape. This marked the beginning of a more structured land defense system in the Kingdom.

1929

Formation of the Regular Army

In 1929, King Abdulaziz ordered the establishment of the first modern regular army units. These included infantry forces, artillery units, and machine gun units, forming the core of the Kingdom’s modern land forces.

1930s–1950s

Military Administration and Training

Later, the Military Affairs Administration and the Defense Agency were established to strengthen military organization. Military education also developed through specialized training institutions, most notably the military school in Makkah and later in Taif.

1970s

Expansion

The Land Forces expanded through modernization programs, better equipment, and improved military infrastructure, allowing them to respond more effectively to the Kingdom’s growing defense needs.

2000s

Modernization

Advanced armored systems, modern weapons, and tactical training greatly strengthened the combat readiness and operational capabilities of the Royal Saudi Land Forces.

Challenges

Geographical & Strategic Challenges

The Kingdom of Saudi Arabia spans over two million square kilometers, making the mission of the Royal Saudi Land Forces highly complex due to geographical and strategic factors.

  • Extensive borders with countries facing varying political conditions.
  • Protection of vital oil facilities that are among the most important in the world.
  • Safeguarding Makkah and Madinah, which hold great religious and political significance.
