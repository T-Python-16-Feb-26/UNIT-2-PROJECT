Overview

Royal Saudi Naval Forces

The Royal Saudi Naval Forces are one of the main branches of the Saudi Armed Forces, responsible for defending the security and safety of the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia, its territory, and its territorial waters, while protecting the Kingdom’s maritime interests and securing its coastlines.

The Naval Forces operate two major fleets: the Eastern Fleet in the Arabian Gulf and the Western Fleet in the Red Sea. Each fleet is supported by an integrated military structure that includes combat ship units, administrative and technical support units, naval aviation groups, marine forces, and special maritime security units.

Through advanced warships, patrol vessels, surveillance systems, and naval bases, the Royal Saudi Naval Forces monitor maritime activity, defend territorial waters, and support joint operations with the other branches of the armed forces.

Through modernization, continuous training, and strategic development, the Naval Forces continue to strengthen their capabilities in protecting ports, coastal facilities, and key sea routes that are vital to the Kingdom’s stability, security, and economy.
