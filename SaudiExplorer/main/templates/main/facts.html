Agricultural Facts & Stats

Surprising, fascinating, and inspiring facts about Saudi Arabia's remarkable journey in agriculture.

Saudi Arabia: A Desert That Feeds a Nation

Despite being 95% desert, the Kingdom has built one of the most innovative agricultural sectors in the world through ingenuity, technology, and investment.

🌍
01
🌴

World's Date Powerhouse

Saudi Arabia is consistently among the top 3 global producers of dates, producing over 1.5 million metric tons annually. The country has more than 3 million date palms, and dates have been cultivated in the Arabian Peninsula for over 7,000 years.

02
💧

Fossil Water Crisis

Saudi Arabia sits atop the Saq Aquifer, one of the world's largest fossil water reserves — water that has been underground for 20,000+ years. Intensive wheat farming in the 1980s–90s drew these non-renewable reserves at an alarming rate, leading to policy reforms that now prioritize water conservation.

03
🌾

The Wheat Miracle (and Lesson)

In the 1980s, Saudi Arabia achieved the seemingly impossible: full wheat self-sufficiency and even became a wheat exporter. At peak production, the Kingdom grew over 4 million tons per year in the desert using center-pivot irrigation — one of the most ambitious agricultural experiments in history.

04
🌹

The World's Most Expensive Roses

The Taif Rose (Rosa damascena) is one of the world's most prized flowers. It takes approximately 2.5–5 tons of rose petals to produce just 1 kilogram of Taif rose oil, making it more valuable than gold by weight. Taif rose water and attar are used in luxury perfumes worldwide.

05
🫒

Record-Breaking Olive Farm

Al-Jouf Agricultural Development Company operates one of the world's largest olive farms, with over 3 million olive trees in Saudi Arabia's northwestern desert. The farm covers hundreds of thousands of hectares and produces millions of liters of olive oil annually for domestic consumption and export.

06
☀️

Solar-Powered Farming

Saudi Arabia is pioneering solar-powered agriculture, where the same land hosts both solar panels and crops (agrivoltaics). The panels provide shade that reduces water evaporation, while the crops benefit from cooler microclimate. This dual use maximizes both energy and food output from arid land.

07
🐪

Camel Dairy Industry

Saudi Arabia has a thriving camel dairy industry, with over 1 million camels used for milk and meat production. Camel milk is prized for its nutritional properties and is used in beverages, chocolates, and cosmetics. Saudi camel milk products are exported to over 50 countries globally.

08
🐟

Aquaculture Expansion

Saudi Arabia has massively expanded its aquaculture sector, aiming for 600,000 tons of fish production annually by 2030. The Kingdom is developing large-scale fish farms in Jazan, Yanbu, and NEOM, including land-based salmon farms that use recirculating aquaculture systems (RAS) technology.

09
🍯

Premium Saudi Honey

Sidr honey from southwestern Saudi Arabia is among the world's most expensive honeys, selling for up to $200 per kilogram internationally. Produced by bees that feed exclusively on the Sidr (Ziziphus) tree, the honey is prized for its distinctive flavor and medicinal properties in traditional Arabian medicine.

10
🏙️

NEOM's Agricultural Vision

NEOM, the futuristic mega-city being built in northwestern Saudi Arabia, includes plans for advanced indoor vertical farms, aquaponics systems, and AI-driven agriculture that will feed its residents using minimal water and land. These farms will use 100% renewable energy and produce zero agricultural waste.

11
☕

Ancient Arabian Coffee

Long before Starbucks, Yemen and southwestern Saudi Arabia's Faifa mountains in Jazan were producing some of the world's earliest traded coffee. Arabian Qahwa (coffee with cardamom and saffron) is deeply embedded in Saudi culture. The revival of Saudi specialty coffee is now a growing industry.

12
📈

Vision 2030 Agriculture Goals

Under Vision 2030, Saudi Arabia aims to achieve 60% food self-sufficiency in strategic crops, develop 450,000 hectares of new farmland, reduce food imports by 30%, and establish the Kingdom as a regional hub for agricultural technology and food security innovation.
