Reducing Carbon Emissions

Saudi Arabia aims to reduce emissions by 278 million tons of CO2 equivalent annually by 2030 while maintaining its leadership in the energy sector.

That transition is being supported through renewable energy expansion, energy efficiency programs, and carbon capture technologies designed to lower emissions across major industries.

  • Large-scale renewable projects are expanding national clean energy capacity.
  • Carbon capture systems are being developed to reduce emissions from critical sectors.
  • The Circular Carbon Economy framework guides reduction, reuse, recycling, and removal efforts.

278 MtCO2e

Annual emissions reduction goal by 2030.

4R

Reduce, reuse, recycle, and remove carbon emissions.

Energy Shift

Cleaner power, stronger efficiency, and lower industrial emissions.

Greening the Kingdom

Saudi Arabia is pursuing one of the largest afforestation efforts in the world, with a long-term plan to plant 10 billion trees and rehabilitate more than 74 million hectares of land.

These efforts are intended to restore natural ecosystems, combat desertification, improve air quality, and strengthen environmental resilience across the Kingdom.

10 Billion Trees

A national-scale planting ambition designed to expand vegetation cover and support long-term climate resilience.

74 Million Hectares

A major land rehabilitation target focused on restoring degraded landscapes and protecting ecosystems.

Protecting Land and Sea

Saudi Arabia is working to protect 30% of its land and marine areas by 2030, helping preserve habitats, strengthen biodiversity, and secure natural systems for future generations.

This includes expanding protected areas, restoring ecosystems, and safeguarding wildlife across both terrestrial and marine environments.

Expand national conservation zones on land and at sea.

Restore fragile habitats and support long-term ecological balance.

Safeguard species and preserve biodiversity for future generations.
