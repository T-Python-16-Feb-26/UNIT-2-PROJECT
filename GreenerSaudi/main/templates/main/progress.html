Scaling Renewable Energy and Emissions Reduction

Saudi Arabia has accelerated its shift toward cleaner energy and lower emissions. Renewable energy capacity is growing through large-scale solar and wind projects, supporting the national target of generating 50% of electricity from renewables by 2030.

At the same time, the Kingdom is investing in advanced carbon capture technologies, with plans to capture up to 44 million tons of CO2 annually by 2035.

5%

Current renewable energy share in Saudi Arabia's energy mix.

Nationwide Reforestation and Land Restoration

Large-scale environmental restoration efforts are already underway across the country. Since the launch of the Saudi Green Initiative, more than 40 million trees have been planted, contributing to the long-term goal of planting 600 million trees by 2030.

These projects are helping combat desertification, restore degraded land, and improve biodiversity across deserts, wetlands, and coastal areas.

40 million

Trees planted so far across Saudi Arabia.

Timeline of Progress

2016

Launch of the King Salman Renewable Energy Initiative to kickstart large-scale renewable energy development in Saudi Arabia.

2017

Announcement of the National Renewable Energy Program to expand clean energy capacity and diversify the energy mix.

2018

Launch of the National Environment Strategy aimed at protecting ecosystems and improving environmental sustainability nationwide.

2019

Establishment of the Special Forces for Environmental Security to enforce environmental protection and combat ecological violations.

2020

“Let’s Make It Green” campaign announced to reduce desertification and increase environmental awareness and action.

2021

Saudi Green Initiative Forum, Middle East Green Summit, and Youth Green Summit launched to unify climate action efforts.

2022

SGI Forum and MGI Summit held alongside COP27 in Sharm El Sheikh, showcasing Saudi Arabia’s climate commitments.

2023

SGI Forum and SGI Talks hosted at COP28 in Dubai within the Saudi Green Initiative Gallery.

2024

SGI Forum held on the sidelines of UNCCD COP16 in Riyadh, reinforcing global land restoration discussions.

2030

Aim to achieve NDC reducing emissions by more than 278 mtpa, protect 30% of land and sea, and plant 600+ million trees

2060

National goal to achieve net-zero carbon emissions across Saudi Arabia.
