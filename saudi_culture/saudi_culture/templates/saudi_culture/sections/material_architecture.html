Architecture

Building Between Desert and Sky

← Material Culture

Saudi architecture tells the story of adaptation and ambition. The traditional Najdi style — with its thick mud-brick walls, geometric parapets, and narrow winding streets — was built to withstand the desert heat. Homes in Jeddah's Al-Balad district feature ornate wooden Rawasheen (bay windows) that catch the coastal breeze. The Asir region in the southwest is strikingly different: multi-storey stone houses decorated with white gypsum bands and painted interiors. Today, Saudi Arabia's skyline is being redefined by mega-projects like NEOM and the Murabba, blending futuristic vision with deep cultural symbolism.
