Pottery & Ceramics

Clay Shaped by Millennia

← Heritage Gallery

Pottery has been produced in the Arabian Peninsula for over 5,000 years. Archaeological sites across Saudi Arabia — from Tayma in the north to Najran in the south — have yielded painted vessels and incense burners that trace trade connections to Mesopotamia, Egypt, and the Indus Valley. Traditional Saudi pottery uses local clays fired in open kilns, producing unglazed, earth-toned vessels. Today, Saudi potters blend ancestral techniques with contemporary design, creating pieces that honor tradition while speaking to modern aesthetics.
