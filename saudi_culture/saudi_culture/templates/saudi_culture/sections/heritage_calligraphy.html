Arabic Calligraphy

Where Words Become Art

← Heritage Gallery

Arabic calligraphy is considered the highest form of visual art in the Islamic world. In Saudi Arabia, it adorns mosques, palaces, coins, and everyday objects. Major styles include Kufic (angular, monumental), Naskh (flowing, readable, the basis of modern print), and Thuluth (ornate, used on the Saudi flag). Today, Saudi artists push calligraphy into contemporary art — merging traditional scripts with digital media, street art, and architectural installations.
