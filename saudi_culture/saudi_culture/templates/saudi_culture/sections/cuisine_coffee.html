The Coffee Ritual

Gahwa — A Language of Respect

← Saudi Cuisine

Saudi coffee (Gahwa Saudiya) is far more than a drink — it is a language of respect. The beans are lightly roasted to a golden color, ground with cardamom and sometimes saffron or cloves, then brewed in a Dallah (a long-spouted brass pot). The host pours with the left hand into small cups (Finjan). The guest accepts with the right hand, drinks in small sips, and shakes the cup when finished. Refusing is considered an insult. Gahwa is always accompanied by dates.
