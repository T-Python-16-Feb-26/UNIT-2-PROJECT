Sadu Weaving

Patterns Woven into Heritage

← Heritage Gallery

Sadu is the traditional Bedouin weaving art practiced by women of the Arabian Peninsula. Using a ground loom, weavers create bold geometric patterns in striking colors — black, red, orange, and white — each motif carrying symbolic meaning. Triangles represent tents or mountains; diamonds symbolize eyes or water wells. In 2020, UNESCO inscribed Sadu weaving on the Representative List of Intangible Cultural Heritage. Today, Saudi women are reviving this ancient craft through workshops, exhibitions, and contemporary design.
