Traditional Jewelry

Silver Stories of Identity

← Heritage Gallery

Bedouin jewelry served as wearable wealth — entire family fortunes were carried as silver necklaces, bangles, and headpieces. Each piece encoded tribal identity: specific patterns, stones, and techniques identified the wearer's clan, region, and marital status. The Hirz (amulet necklace) contained miniature Quran cases for spiritual protection. Coral from the Red Sea, turquoise from trade routes, and amber from the Baltics adorned the most prestigious pieces. Contemporary Saudi jewelers reimagine these traditions with modern materials.
