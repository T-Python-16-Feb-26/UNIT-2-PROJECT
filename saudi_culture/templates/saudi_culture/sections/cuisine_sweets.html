Sweets & Breads

The Sweetness of Arabian Hospitality

← Saudi Cuisine

Saudi sweets balance richness with simplicity. Luqaimat — golden fried dough balls drizzled with date syrup or honey — are a Ramadan staple. Basbousa (semolina cake) is served at every celebration. Mutabbaq, a stuffed pastry that can be sweet or savory, is beloved street food. Traditional breads include Markook (paper-thin flatbread cooked on a dome) and Tamees (soft tandoor bread). Every region has its own sweet specialty tied to specific seasons and celebrations.
