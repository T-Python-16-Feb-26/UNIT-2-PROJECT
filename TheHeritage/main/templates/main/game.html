Saudi Traditional Games

Traditional games in Saudi Arabia show how people enjoyed simple and meaningful moments together. These games were not only for fun, but also helped build friendships and strong social bonds.

Indoor Games

Taq Taq Taqiyah

A group game where children sit in a circle and pass a cap quickly

How to Play
close

How to Play Taq Taq Taqiyah

Game Description:

A fun and fast-paced traditional game that relies on attention and quick reaction. Children sit in a circle while one player walks around them holding a “taqiyah” (or any small cloth). The player secretly drops it behind someone. If the chosen player notices it, they must quickly stand up and chase the first player before they take their spot.

Number of Players:

5 to 15 players (the more, the more fun)

Category:

Suitable for everyone (boys and girls)

Hadarijah

How to Play
close

How to Play Hadarijah

Game Description:

A traditional Saudi children's game based on rhythm and chanting. Players place their hands on the ground while one player moves their finger across them, reciting a chant. The game is fun and helps develop language and interaction skills.

Number of Players:

3 or more players

Category:

Suitable for everyone (boys and girls)

How to Play
close

How to Play Al-Madud

Game Description:

A traditional Saudi girls' game where players create handmade dolls using simple materials like fabric, beads, and sticks. The dolls are used to act out social and family roles, encouraging imagination and role-playing.

Number of Players:

1 or more players (can be played individually or in groups)

Category:

Suitable for girls (especially ages 12 and under)

How to Play
close

How to Play Al-Zagtah

Game Description:

A traditional game played with five small smooth stones. The player throws one stone into the air and quickly picks up the others from the ground before catching the falling stone. It requires focus, speed, and coordination.

Number of Players:

1 or more players (can be played individually or in groups)

Category:

Suitable for everyone (boys and girls)

Al-Kabush

How to Play
close

How to Play Al-Kabush

Game Description:

A traditional game popular in western Saudi Arabia, especially in Makkah. Players place small marbles inside a drawn circle, then take turns throwing a smooth stone from a distance to knock the marbles outside the circle and win them. The game requires accuracy and focus.

Number of Players:

2 or more players

Category:

Suitable for everyone (boys and girls)

Al-Ba'iyah

Team-based chasing game

How to Play
close

How to Play Al-Ba'iyah

Game Description:

A team-based game played with two equal groups, each having a base called “Al-Mawrid.” Players try to enter the opponent's area and return safely while avoiding being tagged. If a player is touched, they must freeze in place until a teammate comes to rescue them. The game involves strategy, speed, and teamwork.

Number of Players:

10 players (divided into two equal teams)

Category:

Suitable for everyone (boys and girls)
