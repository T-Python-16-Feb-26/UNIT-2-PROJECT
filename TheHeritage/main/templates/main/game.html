Saudi Traditional Games

Traditional games in Saudi Arabia show how people enjoyed simple and meaningful moments together. These games were not only for fun, but also helped build friendships and strong social bonds.

Taq Taq Taqiyah

A group game where children sit in a circle and pass a cap quickly

Um Al-Tisa (Nine Men's Morris)

A strategy game where players form lines to win

Al-Danana (Tire Rolling Game)

A game of rolling a tire while running and controlling it

Al-Masaqeel

A marble game where players use skill to hit and win others' marbles

Al-Hajlah (Hopscotch)

Jumping on one foot across drawn squares on the ground

Al-Zagtah

A five-stone game where a player tosses one and quickly picks up the others

Al-Ghumaydah (Hide and Seek)

A hide-and-seek game where one player searches for others

Hadarijah

A group game where children place their hands and one counts until a hand is chosen

Al-Damah

A board game focused on trapping the opponent’s pieces using strategy

Al-Kiram

A board game where players strike discs into corner pockets

Al-Madud

A girls' game where simple dolls are made and used for role-playing

Al-Kabush

A game where players throw a stone to knock marbles out of a circle and win them.

Al-Ba'iyah

A team game where a player enters the opponentطs side and returns; if tagged, they must wait until a teammate frees them.

Al-Sijah

An ancient stone game based on strategy and trapping the opponent, similar to chess

Al-Dibaq

A two-player game where each tries to toss a small piece into a hole from a distance.
