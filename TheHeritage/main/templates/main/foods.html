Saudi Traditional Food

Saudi traditional food reflects the richness of the Kingdom's heritage and culture, featuring distinctive flavors and simple ingredients that have been passed down through generations.

Main Dishes

Kabsa

Rice dish cooked with meat and aromatic spices.

Jareesh

Crushed wheat cooked with meat and spices.

Qursan

Thin bread layered with vegetables and meat stew.

Mataziz

Small dough pieces cooked in a rich meat broth.

Margaouq

Thin dough cooked with vegetables and meat.

Manto

Steamed dumplings filled with spiced meat.

Harees

Wheat and meat cooked until soft and creamy.

Maghsh

Meat and vegetables cooked in a traditional stone pot.

Saleeq

Rice cooked with milk and served with chicken or meat.

Haneeth

Slow-cooked meat in an underground oven.

Sayiadiah

Rice cooked with fish and spices, known for its rich flavor and brown color.

kubaibet-hail

Meat and bulgur balls, seasoned or stuffed, fried or cooked as a traditional dish.

Madini-Rice

Rice cooked with chicken and mild spices, known for its light flavor and soft texture.

Hassawi-Rice

Red rice cooked with spices and meat or chicken, known for its rich flavor and unique texture.

Ful Hijazi

Cooked fava beans served with olive oil and lemon.

Ma'soob

Mashed banana mixed with bread, honey, and cream.

Aseedah

Thick dough served with broth or honey.

Yaghmash

Baked dough filled with minced meat.

Mulyhiya

Traditional dish made with flour and broth.

Al-Mujalalah

Simple dough-based dish with local ingredients.

Mursa

Mashed bread mixed with broth and meat.

Thareed

Bread soaked in rich meat broth.

Ruqsh

Bread mixed with meat and vegetable broth, served as a traditional hearty dish.

Areekah

Dough mixed with dates and honey.
